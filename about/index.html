About

What is HackRice?

HackRice is Rice University's annual hackathon event. Participants trade their sleep for a weekend and join a team to create a project - typically software - in an environment optimized for creativity. At the end of the event, the participants demonstrate their projects to fellow hackers, visitors, and a panel of judges. The event is hosted by the Rice Computer Science Club. This year, Rice University IEEE will also be helping out by providing better support for hardware hacks!

How it all started

Unsatisfied with the lack of self-driven projects by computer science majors at Rice, a group of 5 undergraduates: Waseem Ahmad, Dennis Qian, Joan Chao, Frank Salinas, and Sal Testa decided to organize the first hackathon at Rice. With the support of the Rice Computer Science department, Rice School of Engineering, our amazing sponsors, and enthusiastic community of students, we successfully held our first hackathon during Spring 2012 with over 50 participants including undergradutes, graduates, and alumni accross various disciplines.

Last year, we hosted our first region wide hackathon with students joining us from nearby univerisities and high schools.

This year we will be expanding the hackathon to include even more students from outside the region!
